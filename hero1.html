☘ White Clover, Trifolium repens

White Clover has trifoliate leaves, meaning each leaf is composed of three smaller leaflets, which are oval and typically have a white or pale green mark near the base. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers that are small and form rounded heads.It generally grows low to the ground, spreading along the surface with creeping stems that can root at the nodes. The plant typically reaches a height of 4 to 8 inches

Barley, Hordeum vulgare

Barley is a major cereal grain, known for its elongated seed heads with rows of grain. The plant is characterized by its hollow stems, slender leaves, and fibrous root system. Grown in temperate regions worldwide, it prefers well-drained soils with moderate fertility. Barley is primarily used for animal feed, human food (like barley bread and soups), and in brewing to make beer and whiskey. It is also used as a cover crop.

Watercress, Nasturtium officinale

Watercress is an aquatic or semi-aquatic plant with small, rounded leaves and white flowers. It grows rapidly in cool, flowing water.Found near springs, streams, and rivers, it thrives in clean, slightly alkaline waterWatercress is commonly used in salads, sandwiches, and soups. It's known for its peppery flavor and high nutrient content.

Prickly Pear, Opuntia dillenii

Prickly Pear is a cactus species characterized by its flat, rounded pads covered in spines, and vibrant yellow to red flowers. It produces edible fruits called tunas.Native to arid and semi-arid regions, it thrives in sandy or rocky soils with good drainage.The pads (nopales) and fruits are edible and used in various culinary dishes. The plant also has medicinal uses, particularly in traditional medicine for treating wounds and inflammation.Prickly Pear is drought-resistant and helps prevent soil erosion, making it important for desert ecosystems.

Devil's Puffball

The Devil's Puffball, also known as the common puffball, is a type of fungi with a rounded, whitish-brown fruiting body covered in small spines or warts. When mature, it releases a cloud of spores if compressed. Found in woodlands, grasslands, and on decaying organic matter, it thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soils.While some puffballs are edible when young, the Devil's Puffball is not typically consumed due to potential toxicity. It is mainly known for its unique spore dispersal method.Puffballs play a role in decomposing organic matter, helping recycle nutrients back into the soil.

Lemerocereus

Often referred to as Old Man Cactus due to its white, hair-like spines, Lemaireocereus is a tall, columnar cactus with bluish-green stems and white woolly areoles. It can produce nocturnal flowers.Native to arid regions of Central America, it thrives in dry, sandy, or rocky soils and is well-adapted to drought.Primarily ornamental, this cactus is popular in desert landscaping and as a houseplant. It is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.Lemaireocereus provides habitat and food for various desert animals, including birds and insects.

Pine

Pines are evergreen coniferous trees belonging to the family Pinaceae. They are characterized by their needle-like leaves, which are usually arranged in clusters of 2 to 5.Pine trees are native to most regions of the Northern Hemisphere, including North America, Europe, Asia, and parts of North Africa. They are highly adaptable and can be found in various environments, ranging from temperate forests to mountainous areas.Pine trees vary in size, from small shrubs to tall trees reaching heights of over 260 feet (80 meters). They have a long lifespan, with some species living for hundreds, even thousands of years.

Low Poly Mediterranean

Low poly models of Mediterranean trees, such as olive and cypress, are commonly used in digital design and 3D modeling to create realistic yet efficient representations of these iconic plants. Olive trees are known for their gnarled trunks and silvery-green leaves, which can add an authentic Mediterranean touch to virtual environments. Cypress trees, with their tall, slender profiles and dense foliage, provide vertical interest and a classic Mediterranean aesthetic.

Bamboo

Bamboo, although not native to the Mediterranean, is often incorporated into garden designs for its striking appearance and rapid growth. With its tall, slender stalks and graceful, arching leaves, bamboo can create a sense of height and elegance in a garden setting. It’s valued for its versatility and ability to form dense screens or privacy barriers. In Mediterranean gardens, bamboo can complement the more traditional plants and add an exotic, lush element.

Eucalypt

Eucalyptus trees are known for their distinctive bark and aromatic leaves, and while they are more common in Australian landscapes, they can add a unique element to Mediterranean gardens. Eucalyptus species vary widely, but many have attractive, peeling bark and long, narrow leaves that release a refreshing scent. They can grow quite tall, making them suitable for creating focal points or adding a sense of scale to a garden. Eucalyptus trees are well-adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in the Mediterranean climate,

Oleander

Oleander is a robust, evergreen shrub that thrives in Mediterranean climates, making it a popular choice for gardens in these regions. It features long, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, white, or yellow. Oleander is highly tolerant of drought and poor soil, making it a practical choice for low-maintenance landscaping. Its dense foliage provides excellent privacy screening, and its colorful blooms offer a bright, cheerful contrast to other garden elements.

Shrub

Evergreen shrubs are a staple in Mediterranean gardens due to their ability to retain foliage throughout the year. Plants like lavender not only provide consistent greenery but also offer aromatic benefits and vibrant blooms. Lavender is appreciated for its soothing scent and delicate purple flowers, while rosemary adds a fragrant, culinary touch with its needle-like leaves and small blue flowers.

Purslane

Purslane is a hardy, low-growing succulent that thrives in the arid conditions typical of Mediterranean climates. Known for its fleshy, green leaves and bright, colorful flowers, purslane serves as an attractive ground cover that requires minimal water and maintenance. It spreads quickly, forming a dense mat that can suppress weeds and stabilize soil. Beyond its ornamental value, purslane is also edible, with a tangy flavor and high nutritional content, making it a dual-purpose plant in the garden.

Rosemary

Rosemary is a versatile, evergreen herb native to the Mediterranean region, prized for its fragrant, needle-like leaves and small, pale blue flowers. It is commonly used in both culinary and ornamental applications. In gardens, rosemary’s aromatic foliage and attractive blooms add visual and sensory interest, while its drought tolerance makes it well-suited to the Mediterranean climate. Rosemary can be grown as a low hedge, ground cover, or even as a small shrub, offering flexibility in garden design.
